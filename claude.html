Body text

A lead paragraph sets the tone. It sits one size larger than body, in a lighter weight, with a little more air between lines. Use it once per section.

Regular body copy uses --text-base at 15px with --text-secondary color. It's meant to be comfortable for long reads without competing with headings. Paragraphs breathe with 1.7 line-height.

You can use strong emphasis to pull a word forward, or italic for citations and soft emphasis. Hyperlinks use a bottom border instead of color alone — visible without relying on hue.
